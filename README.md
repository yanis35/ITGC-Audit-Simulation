## Scenario Overview

MediTrust Health is a B2B SaaS digital health platform (300 employees) that processes protected health information (PHI) for 15+ hospital networks across the United States. The organization is preparing for its **first formal SOC 2 Type II audit** and has engaged an internal audit team to conduct a baseline IT General Controls (ITGC) assessment. This simulation captures the end-to-end audit lifecycle — from planning through reporting and remediation — using **COBIT 2019** as the governing control framework.

## Methodology

The audit followed a structured four-phase approach aligned with COBIT 2019:

1. **Plan** — Engagement scoping, risk assessment, control objective selection, and team resourcing.
2. **Execute** — Detailed test procedures (walkthroughs, inspections, re-performance) across 16 control points.
3. **Evaluate** — Evidence evaluation, deficiency identification, and risk rating using COBIT 2019's capability and maturity guidance.
4. **Report** — Findings register, corrective action plan, and executive summary for the Board and Audit Committee.

Control objectives were mapped to COBIT 2019 management practices under the **APO**, **BAI**, **DSS**, and **MEA** domains.
